# Conduct & cases
### Transworld PeopleOps Portal — a guide for the team

This is part of the portal guide. It's written to be read alongside the app —
press the **?** on any page for the same help in a panel. The portal is a
**control room**: it records and checks pay, bonus and compliance evidence;
HumanManager and Remita still pay people. Figures shown in the portal are
provisional records in Naira (₦).

---
### Disciplinary

The disciplinary case desk. People Ops opens and investigates cases; warnings are approved by the COO tier and final warnings / dismissals by the MD or Chairman. Case records are kept as evidence with defined retention.

**Who can use this:** People Ops manages; COO approves warnings; MD/Chairman approve final warning & dismissal.

**What you can do here:**

- **Open a case** — Starts a disciplinary case (the New case form).
- **Open a case record** — Goes to a case to investigate and act.

**Where this sits:** Open → investigate → (suspend if needed) → issue action with the right approval → record response & acknowledgement → close.

**Good to know:**

- No self-approval — the person preparing a case isn't the one who approves the sanction.
- Retention: verbal 12 months, written 18, final written 24 (permanent if regulatory).

---

### Grievances

The grievance case desk — where People Ops handles grievances employees have raised: acknowledge, investigate, record a finding, and close. Appeals are heard separately by the appeal tier.

**Who can use this:** People Ops manages & investigates; the appeal tier hears appeals.

**What you can do here:**

- **Open a grievance** — Goes to a raised grievance to work it.

**Where this sits:** Employee raises (Raise a Grievance) → acknowledged → investigated → finding → close; appeal heard by the appeal tier.

**Good to know:**

- The employee-facing "Raise a Grievance" page is under People; this desk is the handling side.

---

### Report a Concern

The whistleblowing channel — for reporting suspected misconduct, fraud or regulatory breaches. Reports go to the Compliance Officer (the CCO); concerns about senior management route to the Chairman. Use this when you need a protected, formal channel rather than the grievance route.

**Who can use this:** Every employee.

**What you can do here:**

- **Submit a report** — Files a whistleblowing report into the protected channel handled by the CCO.

**Where this sits:** Report → CCO assigns a handler → investigation → outcome → the reporter is acknowledged.

**Good to know:**

- This is separate from grievances and is treated as protected disclosure.
- Senior-management reports are visible only to the Chairman tier, not the normal CCO route.

---

### Whistleblower

The protected channel for handling whistleblowing reports. The Compliance Officer (CCO) sees normal-route reports, assigns a handler, investigates and records the outcome; reports about senior management are restricted to the Chairman tier.

**Who can use this:** The CCO (whistleblower.access); Chairman tier for senior-management reports (whistleblower.exec).

**What you can do here:**

- **Open a report** — Goes to a report to handle it.

**Where this sits:** Concern reported (Report a Concern) → assign handler → investigate → outcome → acknowledge the reporter.

**Good to know:**

- Access is tightly held — this is sensitive, protected material.
- Senior-management reports are visible only to the Chairman tier, not the normal CCO route.

### Disciplinary case

One case in full: the allegation, the investigation, any suspension, the action issued and its approval, the employee's response and the close.

**Who can use this:** People Ops manages; the warning/dismissal step needs the right approver.

**What you can do here:**

- **Record investigation** — Logs investigation notes and evidence.
- **Suspend / lift suspension** — Records a precautionary suspension and its lifting.
- **Issue an action** — Issues the sanction. A warning is approved at COO tier; a final warning or dismissal needs MD/Chairman (discipline.dismiss). (_two people, on purpose_)
- **Record response / acknowledgement / close** — Captures the employee's response, their acknowledgement, and closes the case.

**Good to know:**

- The approval needed steps up with severity: warning → COO; final warning & dismissal → MD/Chairman.
- A sealed case record can satisfy the staff-file CASE_RECORDS slot.

### Grievance case

One grievance in detail: the matter raised, the investigation, the finding, any appeal and the close.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (manage); appeal tier (appeal).

**What you can do here:**

- **Acknowledge** — Confirms receipt to the employee and starts the clock.
- **Record finding** — Logs the investigation outcome.
- **Record appeal** — The appeal tier records the appeal hearing and decision.
- **Close** — Closes the grievance with its outcome.

**Good to know:**

- Appeals are a separate permission and handler from the investigation, on purpose.

---

### New disciplinary case

Opens a new disciplinary case against an employee, recording the allegation and the people involved.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (discipline.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Open case** — Creates the case and takes you to its record.

**Good to know:**

- Be precise in the allegation — it frames the whole case and its retention.

---

### Raise a Grievance

Where any employee formally raises a workplace grievance. You describe the matter and submit it; People Ops acknowledges and investigates, and there's an appeal route if you're not satisfied with the outcome.

**Who can use this:** Every employee.

**What you can do here:**

- **Submit grievance** — Files your grievance for People Ops to acknowledge and investigate.

**Where this sits:** Raise → acknowledged → investigated → finding → close, with an appeal heard separately.

**Good to know:**

- This is the named, formal route. To report misconduct confidentially, use Report a Concern instead.

---

### Whistleblower report

One report in detail, handled within the protected channel: assignment, investigation, outcome and reporter acknowledgement.

**Who can use this:** The CCO; Chairman tier for senior-management matters.

**What you can do here:**

- **Assign handler** — Assigns who investigates.
- **Record investigation / outcome** — Logs the investigation and its result.
- **Acknowledge reporter** — Closes the loop with the reporter, preserving protection.

**Good to know:**

- Handle with care — confidentiality is the point of the channel.
