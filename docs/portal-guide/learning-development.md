# Learning & development
### Transworld PeopleOps Portal — a guide for the team

This is part of the portal guide. It's written to be read alongside the app —
press the **?** on any page for the same help in a panel. The portal is a
**control room**: it records and checks pay, bonus and compliance evidence;
HumanManager and Remita still pay people. Figures shown in the portal are
provisional records in Naira (₦).

---
### Learning & Development

The learning catalogue and the firm's training record — modules staff can take, the policy handbook to read and acknowledge, and the record of who has completed what. People Ops curates the catalogue; managers can recommend modules; everyone can self-enroll.

**Who can use this:** Everyone can view & enroll; People Ops manages the catalogue; managers recommend.

**What you can do here:**

- **Self-enroll** — Adds yourself to a module.
- **Manage modules** — Creates, edits and uploads materials for modules.

**Where this sits:** Catalogue (modules + handbook) → enroll / be recommended → complete → it shows in My Learning and the compliance record.

**Good to know:**

- Mandatory modules and the handbook acknowledgement matter for compliance — they're not optional reading.

---

### Employee Handbook

The firm's Employee Handbook to read and formally acknowledge. Your acknowledgement is recorded for compliance.

**Who can use this:** Everyone reads & acknowledges; People Ops edits (learning.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Acknowledge handbook** — Records that you've read and accept the current handbook.

**Good to know:**

- Re-acknowledgement may be required when the handbook is updated.

---

### My Learning

Your personal learning record — modules you're enrolled in, what you've completed, and anything outstanding or recommended for you.

**Who can use this:** Every employee, for themselves.

**What you can do here:**

- **Self-enroll** — Joins a module from the catalogue.
- **Update my record** — Marks your own progress; managers/People Ops can update others (learning.manage).

**Good to know:**

- Outstanding mandatory items show here — clear them.

---

### Recommend modules

Lets a manager recommend specific learning modules to their people — nudging development without making it mandatory firm-wide.

**Who can use this:** Managers (learning.recommend).

**What you can do here:**

- **Recommend modules** — Suggests modules to selected staff; the recommendation shows in their My Learning.

**Good to know:**

- A recommendation is a nudge; mandatory assignment is a People-Ops/catalogue setting.

---

### Edit handbook

The editor for the Employee Handbook content that staff read and acknowledge.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (learning.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Save handbook** — Publishes the handbook content (may prompt re-acknowledgement).

**Good to know:**

- A material change should trigger a fresh acknowledgement round.

---

### Learning module

One module in detail — its description, materials, and who's enrolled or completed. Enroll yourself, or (for People Ops) manage its content and records.

**Who can use this:** Everyone views & enrolls; People Ops manages content & records.

**What you can do here:**

- **Self-enroll** — Adds you to the module.
- **Upload / remove material** — Manages the module's materials.
- **Record acknowledgement** — Logs completion / acknowledgement for staff.

**Good to know:**

- Removing a module's material doesn't erase completion records already logged.

---

### New module

Creates a new learning module for the catalogue.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (learning.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Create module** — Adds the module so staff can enroll.

**Good to know:**

- Decide up front whether it's mandatory and for which grades/roles.

---

### Edit module

The form to edit a learning module's details and settings.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (learning.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Save module** — Writes the module definition.

**Good to know:**

- Marking a module mandatory changes who must complete it.
