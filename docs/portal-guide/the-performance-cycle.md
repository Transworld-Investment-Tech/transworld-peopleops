# The performance cycle
### Transworld PeopleOps Portal — a guide for the team

This is part of the portal guide. It's written to be read alongside the app —
press the **?** on any page for the same help in a panel. The portal is a
**control room**: it records and checks pay, bonus and compliance evidence;
HumanManager and Remita still pay people. Figures shown in the portal are
provisional records in Naira (₦).

---
### My Performance

Your own performance space across the calendar-year cycle: your goal agreement, your self-assessment, your mid-cycle input and any development plan or PIP. This is where you contribute your side of the appraisal.

**Who can use this:** Every employee, for themselves.

**What you can do here:**

- **Draft & submit goals** — Builds your goal sheet and submits it for your manager to agree and seal.
- **Acknowledge agreement** — Confirms the sealed goal agreement with your manager.
- **Save self-assessment** — Records your own assessment for the cycle.
- **Submit mid-cycle / acknowledge PIP** — Adds your mid-cycle input in July, or acknowledges a performance-improvement plan.

**Where this sits:** Goal-setting (January) → mid-cycle (July) → year-end self + manager review (Nov–Dec) → calibration (Feb–Mar) → bonus (April).

**Good to know:**

- The cycle is the calendar year (Jan–Dec) — January goals, July mid-cycle.
- Once your manager seals the goal agreement it's locked evidence; later changes go through amendments.

---

### My Team

A line manager's view of their direct reports' performance: the goal agreements to review and seal, progress to note, and the year-end picture. This is where you agree goals with your team and keep them moving.

**Who can use this:** Line managers (performance.team).

**What you can do here:**

- **Request changes** — Sends a report's draft goals back with comments.
- **Approve & seal goals** — Agrees and locks the goal agreement — it becomes immutable evidence. (_permanent once done_)
- **Note progress / add amendment** — Records progress against goals, or amends a sealed agreement through the audited amendment route.

**Where this sits:** Sits opposite My Performance: the employee drafts, the manager agrees and seals, both track through the year.

**Good to know:**

- Sealing is deliberate and permanent — use amendments for later change, not a re-seal.

### Performance

The performance-cycle control room: the appraisal cycles, who's where in them, and the appraisal worksheets. People Ops opens the cycle and starts appraisals; managers and employees fill their sides; calibration agrees the final ratings that feed the bonus.

**Who can use this:** Viewable by managers and oversight; People Ops manages the cycle (performance.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Create / start cycle** — Opens an appraisal cycle and starts appraisals for the population.
- **Open an appraisal** — Goes to one person's appraisal worksheet for the cycle.

**Where this sits:** Calendar-year cycle: goals (Jan) → mid-cycle (Jul) → year-end self + manager (Nov–Dec) → calibration (Feb–Mar) → bonus (Apr).

**Good to know:**

- The cycle is the calendar year — the PD Guides are the source of truth on this.
- Ratings only become final through calibration, which writes the agreed score back.

---

### Calibration

Where the firm agrees final ratings fairly across people, before they drive reward. People Ops opens a calibration session, records the agreed rating per person, and finalizes — which writes the agreed rating back onto the appraisal as the official figure.

**Who can use this:** People Ops runs calibration (performance.manage); oversight can view.

**What you can do here:**

- **Open calibration** — Starts a calibration session for a cycle.
- **Record an entry** — Captures the agreed rating for a person.
- **Finalize** — Locks the session and writes the agreed ratings back to the appraisals. (_permanent once done_)

**Where this sits:** Feb–March, after year-end reviews and before the April bonus. Finalized ratings are what the bonus multiplier uses.

**Good to know:**

- Finalizing writes back and seals — it's the official rating from then on.
- Calibration is the fairness step; the worksheet figure is only indicative until here.

### Improvement plans (PIP)

Performance-improvement plans — the structured, time-boxed plans for staff who need to lift performance. Managers open and steward a PIP; the employee acknowledges it and works it.

**Who can use this:** Managers / People Ops open and manage; the employee acknowledges.

**What you can do here:**

- **Open a PIP** — Starts a plan with objectives and a timeframe (the New PIP form).
- **Open a plan** — Goes to a PIP to update or review it.

**Where this sits:** Open → employee acknowledges → tracked to its review date → outcome. A PIP outcome can feed the staff file.

**Good to know:**

- A PIP is supportive and structured, not a disciplinary sanction — keep the two separate.

---

### Mid-cycle Reviews

The July check-in halfway through the calendar-year cycle. People Ops opens the round; employees submit their own mid-cycle input; managers record the review. It's the "are we on track?" moment before the year-end appraisal.

**Who can use this:** Employees submit their own; managers and People Ops record reviews.

**What you can do here:**

- **Open mid-cycle round** — Managers / People Ops open the round for the population.
- **Submit my mid-cycle** — An employee adds their own mid-cycle input.
- **Record review** — A manager / People Ops records the mid-cycle review for a report.

**Where this sits:** Sits between goal-setting (Jan) and year-end (Nov–Dec); a course-correction, not a rating.

**Good to know:**

- Mid-cycle is in July — the calendar-year cycle, per the PD Guides.

---

### New performance cycle

Opens a new appraisal cycle (normally the calendar year) for the firm.

**Who can use this:** People Ops (performance.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Create cycle** — Creates the cycle so appraisals can be started against it.

**Good to know:**

- Align the cycle to the calendar year — Jan goals, Jul mid-cycle, Apr bonus.

---

### Team member performance

One report's performance from the manager's side: their goals, progress and the actions you can take to agree and steward them.

**Who can use this:** The person's line manager (performance.team).

**What you can do here:**

- **Request changes / approve & seal** — Reviews the draft and seals the agreement (immutable once sealed). (_permanent once done_)
- **Note progress / amend** — Records progress or files an amendment to a sealed agreement.

**Good to know:**

- You only see and act on your own reports here.

---

### Appraisal worksheet

One person's appraisal for one cycle: the self-assessment, the manager review, the scorecard scoring and the indicative multiplier. This is where the year's rating is built before calibration.

**Who can use this:** Managers and People Ops (performance.manage to record).

**What you can do here:**

- **Save self / review** — Records the self-assessment and the manager's review against the scorecard.
- **Unsubmit self** — Reopens a submitted self-assessment for correction.

**Where this sits:** Feeds calibration, which agrees the final rating; the agreed rating then drives the bonus multiplier.

**Good to know:**

- The indicative multiplier here is not final — calibration agrees and writes back the real one.
- The integrity gate can zero a multiplier regardless of the competency score.

---

### New PIP

Opens a new performance-improvement plan for an employee, with objectives and a review date.

**Who can use this:** Managers / People Ops (performance.manage).

**What you can do here:**

- **Open PIP** — Creates the plan and notifies the employee to acknowledge.

**Good to know:**

- Set clear, measurable objectives and a realistic review date.

---

### PIP detail

One improvement plan in full: objectives, check-ins, the employee's acknowledgement and the outcome.

**Who can use this:** Managers / People Ops update; the employee acknowledges.

**What you can do here:**

- **Update PIP** — Records progress, check-ins and the outcome.
- **Acknowledge PIP** — The employee confirms they've seen and understood the plan.

**Good to know:**

- The employee's acknowledgement is recorded — it matters for fairness and the record.
